① The verb goes LAST
English says "I eat sushi". Japanese says "I sushi eat" — the verb always comes at the end. Once you feel this rhythm, everything clicks 😎
私は寿司を食べます。Watashi wa sushi o tabemasu — I eat sushi
コーヒーを飲みます。Kōhī o nomimasu — I drink coffee
② Particles are little name tags
Tiny words that tag the word before them: は (topic — "as for..."), を (the thing you act on), に (destination), で (where it happens).
東京に行きます。Tōkyō ni ikimasu — I go to Tokyo
レストランで食べます。Resutoran de tabemasu — I eat at a restaurant
③ です & ます = polite mode
End your sentences with です (desu) or 〜ます (-masu) and you're automatically polite. It's the safe default with anyone you just met — you literally can't go wrong with it 👍
学生です。Gakusei desu — I'm a student
日本語を勉強します。Nihongo o benkyō shimasu — I study Japanese
④ Questions are easy: just add か
No flipping words around like English! Take any sentence and add か (ka) at the end. That's it. That's the whole rule 🎉
これは寿司ですか？Kore wa sushi desu ka? — Is this sushi?
駅はどこですか？Eki wa doko desu ka? — Where is the station?
⑤ Past tense: でした & ました
Swap the ending: です → でした, 〜ます → 〜ました. Done — you can talk about yesterday. (Bonus: Japanese has no future tense. Tomorrow uses the same form as today. One less thing to learn 😄)
おいしかったです。Oishikatta desu — It was delicious
京都に行きました。Kyōto ni ikimashita — I went to Kyoto
⑥ Survival power phrases
Three patterns that carry a whole trip: 〜をください (please give me ~), 〜はどこですか (where is ~?), 〜てもいいですか (may I ~?).
水をください。Mizu o kudasai — Water, please
トイレはどこですか？Toire wa doko desu ka? — Where's the bathroom?
写真を撮ってもいいですか？Shashin o totte mo ii desu ka? — May I take a photo?
🎵 Japanese words have a melody!
Every syllable is either High or Low. Same sounds + different melody = totally different word 😲
The mic can't hear melody (yet 😅), so here's the trick: tap 🔊 to hear the word in a short sentence, listen closely, and copy the tune — karaoke style 🎤
Don't stress: context saves you 99% of the time. Copying the melody just makes you sound extra natural!
